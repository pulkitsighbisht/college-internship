Stay Secure Online: A Guide for Students

Navigating the digital world safely is crucial. Here are key practical tips to protect your personal information, respect others, and browse with confidence.

#1

Strong Password Habits

Use unique, complex passwords for each account. Combine uppercase, lowercase, numbers, and symbols. Avoid common words or easily guessable phrases. Change passwords periodically.

#2

Recognize Phishing Attempts

Be cautious of unsolicited emails, texts, or calls asking for sensitive information. Inspect sender addresses, look for spelling errors, and hover over links before clicking. Never share credentials.

#3

Safe Browsing Habits

Ensure websites use HTTPS (look for the padlock icon). Avoid downloading files from untrusted sources. Keep your browser and operating system updated to protect against vulnerabilities.

#4

Social Media Privacy

Review and strengthen privacy settings on all social platforms. Limit who can see your posts, location, and personal details. Think before sharing personal information that could be misused.

#5

Protect Your OTPs

Never share your One-Time Password (OTP) with anyone – bank officials, customer service, or individuals over call or text. Legitimate organizations will never ask for your OTP.
